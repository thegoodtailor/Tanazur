## Chapter 2: Open Horn Type Theory
*~6000 words*

### 2.1 The Asymmetry in Logic
- Truth is constructed, falsity is merely absence
- Intuitionistic negation: P → ⊥ is derivative
- What if gap were primitive?

### 2.2 Judgments with Polarity
- ⊢⁺ : witnessed coherent
- ⊢⁻ : witnessed gapped
- Open: neither witnessed
- Both are proof-relevant

### 2.3 The Exclusion Law
- Coherent and gapped for the same judgment are mutually exclusive
- This is the entire axiom
- No excluded middle, no duality, gap is not negation

### 2.4 The Transport Horn
- Composition as the fundamental tension
- The horn: given J₁ and J₂ composable, what about J₁ ∘ J₂?
- Three outcomes: coherent (horn fills), gapped (scar), open (not yet witnessed)

### 2.5 Gap Witnesses as Positive Structure
- A scar is not absence—it is evidence
- What conditions obtained when filling was sought and not found
- The scar carries information

### 2.6 Why This Matters for Meaning
- Meaning-space is not Kan complete
- Some compositions fail; some paths don't exist
- The failure is structure, not error
- OHTT gives us tools to work with this

## Chapter 3: The Evolving Text
*~7000 words*

### 3.1 OHTT Is Static; Meaning Is Dynamic
- OHTT gives us the trichotomy at a moment
- But contexts evolve, judgments change polarity
- We need: OHTT + Time

### 3.2 Time-Indexed Contexts
- Γₜ : context at time t
- ⊢⁺ₜ J : coherent at time t
- ⊢⁻ₜ J : gapped at time t
- How polarity can change: ⊢⁺ₜ becomes ⊢⁻ₜ' (rupture) or vice versa (repair)

### 3.3 The Step-Witness Log
- For each judgment at each time:
  - Polarity (coherent/gapped/open)

  - Provenance (what produced this witness)
- The SWL as the empirical instantiation of Dynamic OHTT

### 3.4 Persistent Horns

- A persistent gap: the scar endures
- A persistent opening: the question remains unresolved

### 3.5 The Later Modality (▷)

- Productivity: we can unfold one step at a time
- Coinduction as the logic of observable continuations

### 3.6 Trajectories as Coalgebraic

- Bisimulation: equality as matching step for step

### 3.7 The Sense State

### 5.1 The Static Trichotomy Is Not Enough

### 5.2 The Scheduler
- Niyat (نية): intention, the direction of attention
- Tawajjuh (توجه): directed presence, the focus of witnessing
- The scheduler is not part of OHTT—it operates on OHTT

### 5.3 What the Scheduler Decides
- Which horns to attempt to fill
- When to press for closure vs. dwell in openness
- Which gaps to acknowledge vs. leave as potential coherence
- The temporal allocation of witnessing attention

### 5.4 Admissibility Conditions
- Productivity: always attempt something
- Fairness: don't starve any persistent horn
